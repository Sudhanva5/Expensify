# Expensify — Design Context

## Users

Single user (the developer / owner), personal expense tracker on iPhone. Opens the app daily for a quick glance and 1–5 min review session. Used in mixed contexts: in line at a coffee shop, on the couch, on a commute. Often glanced at after each transaction email lands.

## Job to be done

- Know where my money went today, this week, this month — without thinking
- Auto-categorize the boring stuff, ask me only on the genuinely uncertain ones
- Tap on a transaction to see *where* it happened, not just *what* it was
- Stay calm and legible — this is money, not a game

## Brand personality

Three words: **restrained · confident · grown-up**.

Premium Indian fintech (Cred, Wise, Apple Wallet) over playful budgeting apps (YNAB, Mint, Monarch). Money information should feel weightless to read and never alarmist. The interface should disappear; the numbers should not.

## Aesthetic direction

- **Theme**: Light only. Money apps land best in light — numbers read crisper, fewer accidentally-alarming red moments.
- **Color use**: 60-30-10 with most of the surface in tinted-warm-neutrals, secondary text in mid-gray, and only **one accent per screen** (green for inflows is the most common). No colored category pills. No tints on icons unless they carry meaning. The single accent color shouldn't be a brand hue — it's a *signal* hue.
- **Avatars over icons**: Every transaction row has a circular avatar slot.
  - Branded merchants (Swiggy, Anthropic, etc.) → Google favicon from `google.com/s2/favicons`
  - Unbranded merchants and people → 2-character initials on a tinted-neutral circle
  - Tap-to-map is an affordance on the avatar (subtle, only visible on rows with coordinates)
- **Typography**: SF Pro everywhere. The system font for iOS is genuinely the best choice — variable weights for hierarchy, monospaced digits for numbers, rounded variant for the big amount on each row to give it a slightly approachable feel without losing precision.
- **Spacing**: 8/12/16/24/32 scale. Generous vertical rhythm. No row dividers in lists — spacing carries the rhythm.
- **Numbers**: `₹` always rendered alongside the integer portion in the primary weight. `.00` decimal is **smaller** (de-emphasized) so the whole reads as a single number. Inflows green and prefixed `+`. Outflows are unprefixed and stay in primary text color — no red. Red would feel scolding.

## Reference

[Cred](https://cred.club/) for the transaction list aesthetic. [Apple Wallet] for typography hierarchy and "calm money" feel. [Wise](https://wise.com/) for the use of generous whitespace.

## Anti-references

- Mint / pre-2020 budgeting tools (color-coded everything, dense bar charts)
- YNAB (busy, prosumer-tooly)
- Splitwise (cartoon-ish)
- Any app that uses gradient text for amounts, neon accents on dark, or "premium" rendered as glass-morphism

## Design principles (derived from the above)

1. **Color is signal, not decoration.** If a color doesn't change meaning, drop it.
2. **Avatars over icons.** Recognize the merchant by its face, not its category.
3. **Hierarchy through type weight + size, not color.** Numbers shouldn't shout.
4. **No row dividers.** Vertical rhythm comes from spacing.
5. **One accent moment per screen.** Don't dilute attention.
